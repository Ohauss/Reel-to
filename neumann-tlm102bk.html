About the brand

Neumann is a German company founded in 1928 by engineer Georg Neumann and Erich Rickmann. The brand has become synonymous with the highest quality in the world of professional audio technology, especially renowned for its studio microphones, which are considered benchmarks in sound recording.

The first legendary Neumann microphone — the CMV 3 ("Neumann bottle") — was used in radio broadcasting in the 1930s. However, true fame came with the U 47 (1949), a tube microphone with a switchable polar pattern, which became the studio standard for vocals. Later models such as the U 67, U 87 (1967), and KM 84 also became iconic and remain in demand in music and film production to this day.

Today, Neumann, part of the Sennheiser Group, produces:

  • Studio microphones (U 87 Ai, M 149, TLM 103, and others)
  • Studio monitor loudspeakers (KH series, e.g., KH 120, KH 310)
  • Digital solutions for recording and sound processing.
